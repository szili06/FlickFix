Friday the 13th

"Friday 13th." (original title: "Friday the 13th") is a 1980 American horror film directed by Sean S. Cunningham and written by Victor Miller. The film is the first part of the series driven by the iconic monster Jason Voorhees, which was later followed by several sequels and remakes. The story centers around a group of young people camping at a place called Crystal Lake. However, as the young people explore the area more and more, a mysterious killer begins to catch and kill them one by one. The mysteries of Crystal Lake and the identification and motivation of the killer are the main issues of the film. "Friday the 13th." is a classic slasher film that had a significant impact on the horror genre and pop culture in general. The film is famous for its character of the iconic monster, Jason Voorhees, who is one of the most famous and terrifying horror icons. "Friday the 13th." has inspired several sequels, remakes, television series, and other mediums over the years, and the series continues to enjoy popularity and cult status among horror fans today.
