The Dictator

"The Dictator" is a 2012 comedy film written and directed by British comedian and actor Sacha Baron Cohen. One of Cohen's most iconic roles in the film is Adm. Gen. Aladeen, the dictator in charge of the fictitious oil-rich corps. The story centers on the dictator Aladeen, who rules the fictitious Central Asian country of Wadiya. However, when Aladeen travels to New York to attend a UN meeting, he is unexpectedly stripped of his power and reduced to a useless commercial employee. The film's main source of humor is how Aladeen clashes with American culture and customs as he tries to regain his dictatorial power. "The Dictator" has Sacha Baron Cohen's signature style of satire and provocation. Much of the film's source of humor is political and social satire, exploiting the absurdities of dictatorial regimes and some of the cornerstones of American society. Cohen's character creates many provocative and humorous scenes that viewers either love or perhaps sometimes find offensive. Although the film has been criticized by some for its lack of political correctness, and by others for relying too easily on stereotypes, "The Dictator" has gained popularity for its love of humor and absurdity. Sacha Baron Cohen's performance is particularly memorable and many scenes in the film leave a lasting impression on fans of the comedy.
